# Client Research

## Recurring pain patterns

### Local service businesses

- answer most inquiries manually
- lose context between chats
- repeat prices, services, schedules and location details constantly
- appear less professional than they actually are

### Appointment businesses

- spend too much time coordinating availability
- suffer no-shows or weak confirmation habits
- lack clear service and staff visibility
- do not know which services or professionals drive demand

### Commercial teams

- leads enter through multiple channels without structure
- follow-up depends on seller discipline
- owner lacks visibility on lead status
- conversion analysis by source or seller is weak or nonexistent

### Physical local businesses

- business information is fragmented
- promotions, location, hours and menu or catalog are spread across platforms
- users need to do too much work to decide whether to contact or visit

## Common objections

- "I already use WhatsApp."
- "I already have Instagram."
- "I don’t need a big system."
- "My business is small."
- "I just need something simple."

## Reframe logic

- WhatsApp is a channel, not a system
- Instagram can attract attention, but it rarely closes the information gap alone
- small businesses benefit more from clarity because they have less margin for missed leads
- simple is good only if it still creates trust and next-step clarity

## What customers really buy

They are not buying:

- code
- pages
- design trends

They are buying:

- a more serious digital presence
- fewer lost leads
- easier booking or follow-up
- more commercial order
- more measurable sales behavior
